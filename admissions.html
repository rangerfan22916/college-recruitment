Cost & Financial Aid

Cost is important — but the value of an IU education is shown in outcomes: high employment rates, debt management, and support throughout your journey.

More than 80% of students receive financial aid. 50% graduate with little to no student debt.*

How Tuition Works

At IU, tuition is a flat rate. You pay the same tuition for up to a certain number of credit hours (for example, full semester load) regardless of extra upper-level / lower-level courses. Summer courses are billed separately.

Additional Costs

Housing plans, meal plans, books, travel & personal expenses vary depending on lifestyle, choice of residence hall, and meal plan. We provide many options so you can pick what works for you and your budget.

For International Students

International students generally have different fee structures and additional costs. Visit our International Services section for full details.

Financial Aid & Support

Nearly 85% of IU undergraduates receive some form of financial aid. We also offer merit and need-based scholarships, grants, work-study options, and flexible payment plans.

  • Scholarships based on merit and financial need
  • Institutional grants
  • Federal aid (Pell, etc.)
  • Loan options and counseling
